# Oregon TANF Eligibility Requirements

## Legal Authority
- **Primary Rule**: OAR 461-135-0070 - Specific Requirements; TANF
- **Dependent Child**: OAR 461-120-0510 - Dependent Child Requirements
- **Filing Group**: OAR 461-110-0330 - Filing Group; TANF

## Categorical Eligibility (OAR 461-135-0070)

### Eligible Categories

A person may receive TANF benefits if they are:

1. **Dependent Child** - A child who meets the dependent child requirements in OAR 461-120-0510

2. **Caretaker Relative** - A caretaker relative of an eligible dependent child who is:
   - Receiving TANF program benefits for the dependent child, OR
   - Applying for TANF program benefits for the dependent child

3. **Caretaker of Ineligible Child** - A caretaker relative of a dependent child when:
   - The child is ineligible because they are receiving SSI, OR
   - The child is in foster care but expected to return home within 30 days

4. **Pregnant Woman** - A parent whose only child is an unborn child may qualify once the pregnancy has reached the calendar month before the month in which the due date falls

## Dependent Child Definition (OAR 461-120-0510)

A dependent child must meet ALL of the following:

### Age Requirements
- Under 18 years of age, OR
- Under 19 years of age AND regularly attending school full-time

### Marital Status
- Cannot be legally married
- Cannot be legally separated
- Cannot be legally emancipated

### "Regularly Attending School" Definition
Enrollment and attendance in any of the following:
- School in grade 12 or below (including home schooling approved by local school district)
- GED classes in lieu of high school
- Vocational or technical training (including Job Corps) in lieu of high school
- Oregon School for the Deaf

### School Attendance Rules
- Full-time status is determined by the school
- Regular attendance continues during absences for illness, family emergency, or vacation if student intends to return
- Students are considered in attendance for the full month in which they complete or discontinue school

## Filing Group Requirements (OAR 461-110-0330)

A TANF filing group must include:
- A dependent child, OR
- An unborn child

Each sibling of a dependent child is included in the filing group if:
- Less than 18 years of age, OR
- 18 years of age and attending school full time

## Immigration Status Requirements

Applicants must be:
- U.S. citizens, OR
- Qualified immigrants

### Qualified Immigrant Categories
- Lawful permanent residents
- Asylees
- Refugees
- VAWA (Violence Against Women Act) recipients
- T-visa holders (trafficking victims)
- Other qualified statuses as defined by federal law

## Disqualification Periods

### 120-Day Disqualification (OAR 461-135-0070)
A need group is not eligible for TANF for 120 days if a parent or caretaker relative:
- Was separated from their last employment where they were hired to work 100+ hours/month, OR
- Voluntarily reduced work hours at such employment

### Exceptions to 120-Day Disqualification
The disqualification does not apply if the person:
- Is a teen parent returning to school
- Is fleeing domestic violence
- Is pregnant with documented medical complications
- Is unable to work due to disability (30+ days)
- Was separated for "good cause"
- Was laid off from employment

## Tribal TANF Requirements

Special rules apply for:
- Klamath Tribes members in designated counties
- Siletz Tribe members in designated counties

These families must apply through tribal TANF programs or face ineligibility for state TANF.
