# Kentucky TANF (K-TAP) Eligibility Requirements

## Source
- **Regulation**: 921 KAR 2:006 - Technical requirements for KTAP
- **URL**: https://apps.legislature.ky.gov/law/kar/titles/921/002/006/

## Program Definition

"Kentucky Transitional Assistance Program" or "KTAP" means Kentucky's Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) cash assistance program for eligible families with a child living in the home.

## Benefit Group Composition

A "benefit group" means one or more children and may include a specified relative. The benefit group includes:
- Dependent children meeting eligibility criteria
- Eligible parents living in the home
- Eligible siblings
- School-attending children ages 16-18
- Minor teenage parents

## Age Requirements for Children

A child is defined as an individual who is:
1. Age 15 or younger, OR
2. Ages 16-18 in regular full-time school attendance, OR
3. Under 18 and a high school graduate

### School Attendance Verification
- Children ages 16-18 must verify regular school attendance
- Absences due to official school vacations, illness, convalescence, or family emergencies do not break attendance requirements

## Deprivation Requirements

Children must be deprived of parental support or care due to one of the following factors:

### 1. Death of a Parent
Either parent's death qualifies the child for deprivation.

### 2. Absence of a Parent
Physical separation from parent through:

**Voluntary Absence:**
- Divorce
- Desertion (30+ days)
- Birth out-of-wedlock

**Involuntary Absence:**
- Incarceration (30+ days)
- Long-term hospitalization
- Deportation

### 3. Incapacity of a Parent
Physical or mental disability lasting 30+ days that reduces the parent's ability to support the child, verified through medical evaluation.

### 4. Unemployment of at Least One Parent (Two-Parent Households)
For two-parent households, a parent qualifies as unemployed if:
- Employed fewer than 100 hours monthly, OR
- Employment exceeding 100 hours is temporary and surrounded by months below that threshold

**Prior Labor Market Attachment** (Note: This requirement was eliminated in 2023 updates):
- Previously required: $1,000 earned in preceding 24 months

## Citizenship and Immigration Requirements

### U.S. Citizens
KTAP shall be provided only to a United States citizen.

### Qualified Aliens
**Eligible immediately:**
- Qualified aliens who entered before August 22, 1996

**5-year eligibility bar with exceptions:**
Qualified aliens entering on or after August 22, 1996 face a 5-year restriction, except for:
- Refugees
- Asylees
- Trafficking victims
- Military personnel (veterans and active duty)
- Afghan/Iraqi special immigrants

## Residency Requirements

Applicants must be:
- Living in Kentucky voluntarily (not for temporary purposes), OR
- Entered the state seeking employment without receiving TANF assistance from another state

## Living Arrangement Requirements

### Relative Requirement
A child must live with a specified relative:
- Blood relative
- Adoptive parent
- Relative by marriage (even if marriage terminated after the child's birth)

### Absence Rule
Children absent 30+ consecutive days lose eligibility unless good cause exists:
- Medical care
- School attendance
- College attendance
- Foster care placement
- Approved family visits

## Minor Teenage Parents

Special requirements for teenage parents under 18:
- Must pursue high school completion or cabinet-approved alternative education once their child reaches 12 weeks old
- Must reside with parents, legal guardians, adult relatives, or appropriate supervised settings (such as second chance homes or maternity facilities)
- Cabinet assists in locating suitable housing if family arrangements are unavailable or unsafe

## Work Registration

Adult applicants and recipients must register for work pursuant to the Kentucky Works Program (KWP).

## Child Support Cooperation

Applicants must cooperate in establishing paternity and securing child support from absent parents.

**Penalty for Non-Cooperation:**
- 25% benefit reduction

**Good Cause Exceptions:**
- Serious physical/emotional harm risk
- Pending adoptions
- Incest/rape conceptions
- Determined through documented investigation

## Exclusions from Eligibility

### SSI Recipients
Children receiving SSI cannot receive KTAP themselves, but the relative caregiver may qualify if other eligibility criteria are met.

### Strikers
Families are ineligible for benefits during months when the custodial parent is participating in a work strike.

### Drug Felony Convictions
Individuals convicted after August 22, 1996 of drug possession/distribution felonies are ineligible.

### Fraud Bars
Individuals convicted of fraudulently claiming residence in multiple states to receive simultaneous assistance face a 10-year bar.

## Time Limits

### 60-Month Lifetime Limit
KTAP cannot be provided for more than 60 cumulative months to a benefit group that includes an adult or minor teenage parent as head of household.

### Hardship Extensions
Extensions are available for individuals who are:
- Battered or subjected to extreme cruelty
- Disabled with cabinet verification
- Caring for disabled family members
- Grandparents or relatives caring for children who would otherwise enter foster care
- Adults facing insufficient employment opportunities (limited to six additional months)
- Domestic violence survivors receiving exemptions

Benefit groups losing employment within 30 days of reaching the 60-month limit receive a three-month extension.

## Domestic Violence Protections

Survivors receive screening and individualized service plans developed by domestic violence providers. These plans may exempt survivors from:
- Residency requirements
- Child support cooperation
- Time limits
- KWP participation

when compliance would impede escape from abuse or unfairly penalize them.
