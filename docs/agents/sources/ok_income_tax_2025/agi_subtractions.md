# Oklahoma 2025 AGI Subtractions

**Source**: Form 511-NR-Pkt, Pages 16-17; Schedule 511-A
**URL**: https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/tax/documents/forms/individuals/current/511-NR-Pkt.pdf#page=16

## List of AGI Subtractions (Schedule 511-A)

### Line 1: U.S. Government Obligations Interest
Interest on bonds, notes, and other obligations of the U.S. Government may be excluded from Oklahoma AGI.
- Interest from FNMA and GNMA does NOT qualify

### Line 2: Social Security Benefits
Social Security benefits included in Federal AGI shall be subtracted.

### Line 3: Federal Civil Service Retirement (CSRS)
**Exclusion**: 100% of retirement benefits
- Includes survivor benefits paid in lieu of Social Security
- Must be from Federal Civil Service Retirement System (CSRS)
- Cannot exceed amount included in Federal AGI

### Line 4: Oklahoma Government Retirement
**Exclusion**: 100% for qualifying plans
Includes:
- Oklahoma Public Employees Retirement System
- Teachers' Retirement System of Oklahoma
- Oklahoma Law Enforcement Retirement System
- Oklahoma Firefighters Pension and Retirement System
- Oklahoma Police Pension and Retirement System
- County employee retirement systems (19 O.S. Section 951 et seq.)
- Uniform Retirement System for Justices and Judges
- Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Department Retirement Fund
- Oklahoma Employment Security Commission Retirement Plan
- Municipal employee retirement systems

### Line 5: Military Retirement

**Tax Year 2022 and Later**: 100% exclusion of military retirement benefits

**Historical Rates** (per OAC 710:50-15-49):
- 2007-2021: Greater of $10,000 or 75% of federal AGI
- 2006: Greater of $10,000 or 50% of federal AGI

**Reference**: Page 17

### Line 6: Other Retirement Benefits

**CRITICAL UPDATE - HB 2020 (2024 Tax Year Forward)**

| Tax Year | Per-Person Limit |
|----------|-----------------|
| 2021-2023 | $10,000 |
| 2024+ | $20,000 |

**Legislative Authority**: HB 2020 (2023 Session)
- Passed House 90-0 on March 20, 2023
- Increased retirement benefits exemption from $10,000 to $20,000
- Effective for tax year 2024 (returns filed in 2025)
- Source: https://www.okhouse.gov/posts/news-20230324_4

**Qualifying Sources**:
- Individual retirement accounts (IRC Section 408)
- SEP plans (IRC Section 408)
- Employee pension plans (IRC Section 401)
- Employee annuities (IRC Section 403(a) or (b))
- Deferred compensation plans (IRC Section 457)
- Lump-sum distributions (IRC Section 402(e))
- U.S. Retirement Bonds (IRC Section 86)

**Note**: Early distributions from retirement funds due to termination of employment prior to retirement or disability do NOT qualify (typically marked with "1" in box 7 of Form 1099-R).

### Line 7: Railroad Retirement Board Benefits
All qualified U.S. Railroad Retirement Board benefits included in Federal AGI may be excluded.

### Line 8: Oklahoma Oil and Gas Depletion
May be computed at 22% of gross income derived from each Oklahoma property during the taxable year.
Deductible amount = Oklahoma computation minus Federal depletion claimed.

### Line 9: Oklahoma Net Operating Loss (NOL)
Enter carryovers from previous years with loss year(s) noted.

### Line 10: Tribal Member Income
If principal residence is in "Indian Country" (per OAC 710:50-15-2), income may be deducted.

### Line 12: Oklahoma Capital Gain Deduction
Enter from Form 561.

### Line 14: Oklahoma Income Distributed by Electing PTE
Pass-through entity income distribution.
