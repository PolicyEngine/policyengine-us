# Oklahoma 2025 Tax Rates and Brackets

**Source**: Form 511-NR-Pkt, Page 38
**URL**: https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/tax/documents/forms/individuals/current/511-NR-Pkt.pdf#page=38
**Statutory Authority**: 68 O.S. Section 2355

## Tax Year 2025 (Current)

### Single and Married Filing Separately

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $1,000 | 0.25% |
| $1,000 - $2,500 | 0.75% |
| $2,500 - $3,750 | 1.75% |
| $3,750 - $4,900 | 2.75% |
| $4,900 - $7,200 | 3.75% |
| Over $7,200 | 4.75% |

### Married Filing Jointly, Head of Household, and Qualifying Surviving Spouse

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $2,000 | 0.25% |
| $2,000 - $5,000 | 0.75% |
| $5,000 - $7,500 | 1.75% |
| $7,500 - $9,800 | 2.75% |
| $9,800 - $12,200 | 3.75% |
| Over $12,200 | 4.75% |

**Note**: For tax year 2024 and 2025, the top bracket threshold for joint filers is $14,400, per the statutory calculation in 68 O.S. Section 2355(C)(2)(a): $2,000 + $3,000 + $2,500 + $2,300 + $4,600 = $14,400.

## Tax Year 2026 Changes (HB 2764)

Starting in tax year 2026, rates are consolidated to three brackets:

### Single and Married Filing Separately (2026)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $3,750 | 0% |
| $3,750 - $4,900 | 2.5% |
| $4,900 - $7,200 | 3.5% |
| Over $7,200 | 4.5% |

### Married Filing Jointly (2026)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $7,500 | 0% |
| $7,500 - $9,800 | 2.5% |
| $9,800 - $14,400 | 3.5% |
| Over $14,400 | 4.5% |
